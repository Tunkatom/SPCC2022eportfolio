Introduction page

In kindergarten, I got the principal's award. In second grade, I got the "most likely to be next Einstein award". In fourth or fifth grade, I was the male class representative on the student council.

In eighth grade, I entered robotics, and in the second semester of the same year, joined the school fencing club. My robotics team placed first and second in the fall and spring competitions, respectively, and our school, PRMS, took home the overall champion in both competitions. I've been playing piano for six, soon to be seven consecutive years now, with my Christmas recital coming up. These are my accomplishments.

At home, I'm a DIY maker, having made a coil gun, night vision headset, printing press, and Bluetooth headphones. I'm an avid gamer on the PC and slightly outdated Wii. I have one younger brother, and I live with my mother and grandparents.

As before mentioned, I enjoy fencing. Since the eighth grade ended, I've only been able to practice with my brother, but hopefully that will change in the future. I also enjoy soccer, which I play during my lunch period. I usually play as goalie or defense, I'm not one to run the ball upfield and score a goal.

More into school, I'm aiming to become a computer engineer. I currently attend Union County Early College at South Piedmont Community College, so that means I"ll graduate in five years with an Associates in Arts. In my Junior year, I can transfer that to an Associates in Science. Through elementary and middle school, I was always a good student, never getting a D or below on my progress reports or other cards. I had absolutely great teachers that allowed me to be here where I am today. Hats off to them!

I'm a tactile or kinesthetic learner, which means I like to learn by doing, touching, acting, things like that. Close behind is visual, which means I like to see it done, watch the process instead of reading it in a book. The combination of these two means I really like hands-on labs in science class, or projects that involve physically making a poster or object to show my knowledge.
